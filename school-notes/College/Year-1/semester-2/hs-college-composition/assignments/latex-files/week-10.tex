  % Paragraph 2: History
  David Chaum was a cryptographer who was the first person to propose a
  blockchain-like protocol in his $1982$ dissertation, which was named
  "Computer Systems Established, Maintained, and Trusted by Mutually Suspicious
  Groups". Years later, Stuart Haber and W. Scott Stornetta worked on a
  cryptographically secured chain of blocks because they wanted to create a
  system, which the blocks of information couldn't be tampered with. In $1992$,
  they, along with Dave Bayer improved the efficiency by implementing
  \textbf{Merkle Trees}, which encodes the blockchain data in an efficient and
  secure manner into the chain design. Satoshi Nakamoto is an anonymous person
  or group who's credited with the creation of the first decentralized
  blockchain in $2008$. He then improved the blockchain's security by using the
  Hashcash method to timestamp blocks. This way, we don't need any signature
  from a trusted party, which might introduce another problem since it will
  slow down the rate at which blocks are added to the chain. The following
  year, the design was implemented. Today, it servers as a public ledger for
  all transactions on the network. You can imagine the ledger as a spreadsheet
  with all the transaction information. Today, the ledger's size is $389.4$ GB,
  which is almost half a terabyte of transaction information. In $2016$,
  blockchain started to gain fast adaption rate within financial services.
  Finally, in $2019$ Gartner reported $5\%$ of CIOs believed blockchain
  technology was a game-changer for their business.
