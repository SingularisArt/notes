\lesson{4}{Mar 18 2022 Fri (16:49:49)}{Solve tax rate/interest using system of equations}{Unit 2}
\label{les_4_unit_2:solve_tax_rate/interest_using_system_of_equations}

A theater group made appearances in two cities. The hotel charge before tax in
the second city was $\$1500$ lower than in the first. The tax in the
\underline{first city was $3.5\%$}, and the tax in the \underline{second city
was $6\%$}. The hotel tax paid for the two cities was $\$385$. How much was the
hotel charge in each city before tax?

Let's first create our needed variables:

$x$ = the hotel charge (in dollars) in the first city before tax
$y$ = the hotel charge (in dollars) in the second city before tax

Now, let's create a table to help organize all of our data:

\begin{tabular}{|c|c|c|c|}
  \hline
  & Charge before tax & Tax rate & Tax paid \\
  \hline
  First city & $x$ & $3.5\% = 0.035$ & $0.035x$ \\
  \hline
  Second city & $y$ & $6\% = 0.06$ & $0.06y$ \\
  \hline
  Total & & & $385$ \\
  \hline
\end{tabular}

Using this information, we can create our system of equations. 

\begin{equation}
  \label{cse:final_system_of_equations}
  \begin{cases}
    y = x - 1500 \\
    0.035x + 0.06y = 385
  \end{cases}
.\end{equation}

Before we go any further, we need to make the second equation into the form of
$y = mx + b$:

\begin{equation}
  \label{spt:transform_equation_into_y_mx_b}
  \begin{split}
    0.035x + 0.06y &= 385 \\
    \frac{385 - 0.035x}{0.06} &= y
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

Now, we just need to graph this system of equations and find the point of
intersection, which gives us the following solution:

$x = 5000 \qquad y=35000$
