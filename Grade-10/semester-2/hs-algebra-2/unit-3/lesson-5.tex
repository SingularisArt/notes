\lesson{5}{Mar 18 2022 Fri (16:54:08)}{Factor quadratic with LC greater than 1}{Unit 3}
\label{les_5_unit_3:factor_quadratic_with_lc_greater_than_1}

Let's say we have the following quadratic: $2x^{2} + 9x + 9$.

There aren't any common factors in any of the coefficients/variables.
So, we can break it up into $2$ factors.

Here's what we do. We find something that is equal to:

$a \times c = b$. So, this is: $2 \times 9 = 18 = 9$. So, what two numbers
that we multiply produce $18$ and when you add them you get $9$. The answer
is $6$ and $3$. Now, the quadratic can be written as the following:

\[ 2x^{2} + 6x + 3x + 9 \].

Now, we can factor by grouping:

\begin{equation}
  \label{spt:factor_by_grouping_1}
  \begin{split}
    2x^{2} + 6x + 3x + 9 &= (2x^{2} + 6x) + (3x = 9) \\
                         &= 2x(x + 3) + 3(x + 3) \\
                         &= (x + 3)(2x + 3) \\
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

So, that's our final answer: $(x + 3)(2x + 3)$.
