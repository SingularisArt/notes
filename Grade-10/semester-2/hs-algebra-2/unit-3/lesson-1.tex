\lesson{1}{Feb 21 2022 Mon (10:17:30)}{Factoring a sum or difference of two cubes}{Unit 3}

Let's factor:

\begin{align}
  v^3 + 27
.\end{align}

We can use the following formulas to factor the sum or difference of two cubes:

\begin{align}
  \textrm{Sum of two cubes:} \quad a^3 + b^3 = (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2) \\
  \textrm{Difference of two cubes:} \quad a^3 - b^3 = (a - b)(a^2 + ab + b^2)
.\end{align}

We need to factor: $v^3 + 27$.

\begin{remark}[$27$]
  Notice how $27$ can be written as $27 = 3^3$.

  So, $v^3 + 27$ is the sum of two cubes:

  \begin{align}
    v^3 + 27 = v^3 + 3^3
  .\end{align}
\end{remark}

We can use the formula for the sum of two cubes to factor $v^3 + 3^3$:

\begin{equation}
  \begin{split}
    a^3 + b^3 &= (a + b)(a^2 - ab + b^2) \\
            &= (v + 3)(v^2 - v \times 3 + 3^2) \quad
              \textrm{Letting $a = v$ and $b = 3$} \\
            &= \boxed{(v + 3)(v^2 - 3v + 9)}
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}
