\lesson{4}{Mar 18 2022 Fri (16:33:35)}{Graph line through given point with slope}{Unit 1}
\label{les_4_unit_1:graph_line_through_given_point_with_slope}

There are three major equations we can use:

\begin{equation}
  \label{eqt:three_major_equations}
  \begin{split}
    &y = mx + b \\
    &Ax + Bx = C \\
    &y_1 - y = m(x_1 - x)
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

We can use either of these equations. Now, we just plug in the given point and
given slope.

\begin{example}
  \label{exm:graph_line_through_given_point_with_slope}

  We have the point $(6, 2)$ and the slope of $-4$. Let's use the three
  equations above to create an equation and then graph:

  \begin{equation}
    \label{edt:y_mx_b_form}
    \begin{alignedat}{3}
      &\textrm{You can use any of the $3$ equations} \\
      y &= mx + b \\
      2 &= -4(6) + b \qquad\qquad && \textrm{Plugin the $x$ and $y$ values} \\
        & \textrm{Solve for $b$} \\
      -b &= -4(6) - 2 \qquad\qquad && \textrm{Subtract $b$ and $2$} \\
      b &= 4(6) + 2 \qquad\qquad && \textrm{Divide both sides by $-1$} \\
      b &= 26 \qquad\qquad && \textrm{Do basic arithmetic} \\
        & \textrm{Final Equation} \\
      y &= -4x + 26 \\
    \end{alignedat}
  .\end{equation}
\end{example}
