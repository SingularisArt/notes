\lesson{14}{Mar 16 2022 Wed (12:40:50)}{Solve word problem using quadratic equation}{Unit 4}

A ball is thrown from an initial height of $5$ feet with an initial upward
velocity of $36 \frac{ft}{s}$. The ball's height $h$ (in feet) after $t$ seconds
is given by the following quadratic equation: $h = 5 + 36t - 16t^{2}$.

Find all values of $t$ for which the ball's height is $21$ feet.

To find when the ball's height is $21$ feet, we substitute $21$ for $h$ and
solve for $t$. $21 = 5 + 36t - 16t^{2}$. Then, we set the whole thing equal to
$0$. Finally, we use the quadratic formula to solve for $t$:

\begin{equation}
  \label{eqt:solve_for_t}
  \begin{split}
    a &= 16 \\
    b &= -36 \\
    c &= 16 \\
    t &= \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^{2} - 4ac}}{2a} \\
      &= \frac{-(-36) \pm \sqrt{(-36)^{2} - 4 \times 16 \times 16}}{2 \times 16} \\
      &= \frac{36 \pm \sqrt{272}}{32} \\
      \qquad\qquad 0.6096 \qquad&\qquad 1.6403 \\
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

So, the ball's height is $21$ at approximately $0.61$ seconds (on its way up) or
$1.64$ seconds (on its way down).
