\lesson{1}{Mar 04 2022 Fri (18:07:42)}{Domain and range from parabola}{Unit 4}

Let's try to find the domain (\ref{def:domain}) and range (\ref{def:range}) of
this parabola:

\begin{figure}[htpb]
  \centering
  \incfig{domain-and-range-parabola}
  \label{fig:domain_of_parabola_start}
\end{figure}

\begin{itemize}
  \label{item:domain_and_range_of_parabola}

  \item To find the domain, we project the graph onto the $x$ axis.

    \begin{figure}[htpb]
      \centering
      \incfig{domain-of-parabola}
      \label{fig:domain_of_parabola_end}
    \end{figure}

    Because the graph extends to the left and right forever, all real numbers
    are in the domain.

  \item To find the range, we project the graph onto the $y$ axis.

    \begin{figure}[htpb]
      \centering
      \incfig{range-of-parabola}
      \label{fig:range_of_parabola_end}
    \end{figure}

    We see that the minimum value in the range is $-3$.

    All values greater than $-3$ are also part of the range. This is because the
    graph extends upward forever.

    Therefore, the range of our function is given by $y \ge -3$.
\end{itemize}
