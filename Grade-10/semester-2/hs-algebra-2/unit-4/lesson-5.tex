\lesson{5}{Mar 04 2022 Fri (20:56:39)}{Graphing a quadratic inequality}{Unit 4}

To graph an inequality, we first graph the associated parabola.

\begin{example}
  \label{exm:graphing_inequality}

  Graph the inequality $y > -x^{2} + 7$. First you graph $y = -x^{2} + 7.$

  The inequality has  \textbf{y greater than} $-x^{2} + 7$, so we shade
  \textbf{above} the curve.

  If it was \textbf{y less than}, we shade \textbf{below} the curve.

  Since the inequality is only $>$, that means the curve itself is not part of
  the graph. We use a dashed curve to show this.

  If the inequality was $\ge$, then we would use a solid curve.

  So, here's the inequality $y > -x^{2} + 7$ graphed.

  \begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \incfig{inequality}
    \label{fig:inequality}
  \end{figure}
\end{example}
