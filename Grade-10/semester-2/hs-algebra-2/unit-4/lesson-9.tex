\lesson{9}{Mar 16 2022 Wed (11:07:03)}{Graph a quadratic inequality: Problem Type 2}{Unit 4}

To graph the inequality $y \le x^{2} - 10x - 23$, we first graph the associated
parabola $y \le x^{2} - 10x - 23$.

A parabola in the form $y = a(x - h)^{2} + k$ has a vertex $(h, k)$.

We'll rewrite $y = -10x^{2} = 10x - 23$ in this form. We start with the first
two terms:

\begin{equation}
  \label{eqt:graph_inequality_problem_type_2}
  \begin{split}
    y &= -x^{2} - 10x - 23 \\
      &= -(x^{2} + 10x) - 23
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

Now, we need to complete the square of the expression $x^{2} + 10x$.

Since we have $x^{2} + 10x$, we must add $\left(\frac{10}{2}\right)^{2} = 25$
inside and outside the parenthesis. So, our final answer is:

\begin{equation}
  \label{eqt:final_answer_for_graph_inequality_problem_type_2}
  \begin{split}
    y = -(x + 5)^{2} + 2
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

So, now we know that the vertex is at $(-5, 2)$. And we graph the quadratic.
Since we have $y \le$, we shade below the curve since everything in the curve is
less than $y$.
