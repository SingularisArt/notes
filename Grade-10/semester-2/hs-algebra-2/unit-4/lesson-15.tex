\lesson{15}{Mar 16 2022 Wed (12:51:03)}{Find x, y-intercepts given a polynomial}{Unit 4}

Find all $x$-intercepts and $y$-intercepts of the graph of the function:
$f(x) = x^{3} + 5x^{2} - 4x - 20$ 

\begin{enumerate}
  \label{enum:find_x_intercept_for_a_given_polynomial}

  \item To find the $x$-intercepts, we set $f(x) = 0$ and solve for $x$:

    \begin{equation}
      \label{eqt:find_x_intercept_for_a_given_polynomial}
      \begin{alignedat}{3}
        0 &= x^{3} + 5x^{2} - 4x - 20 \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Given} \\
          &= (x^{3} + 5x^{2}) + (-4x - 20) \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Gathering terms} \\
          &= x^{2}(x + 5) - 4(x + 5) \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Factoring out $x^{2}$ and $-4$} \\
          &= (x + 5)(x^{2} - 4) \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Factor $x + 5$ out} \\
          &= (x + 5)(x - 2)(x + 2) \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Final Factored Form} \\
        x &= -5, 2, -2
      \end{alignedat}
    .\end{equation}

    So, the $x$-intercepts are $-5, 2, -2$.

  \item To find the $y$-intercept, we $x = 0$.

    \begin{equation}
      \label{eqt:find_y_intercept_for_a_given_polynomial}
      \begin{alignedat}{2}
        f(x) &= x^{3} + 5x^{2} - 4x - 20 \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Given} \\
        f(0) &= (0)^{3} + 5(0)^{2} - 4(0) - 20 \qquad\qquad &&\textrm{Set $x = 0$} \\
             &= f(0) = -20 \\
      \end{alignedat}
    .\end{equation}
\end{enumerate}

So, our $x$-intercepts are $(-5, 0), (2, 0), (-2, 0)$ and our  $y$-intercept is
$(0, -20)$.
