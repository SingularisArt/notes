\lesson{12}{Mar 15 2022 Tue (21:49:41)}{Solve quadratic equation with roots}{Unit 4}

To find all complex solutions to a quadratic equation, we use the quadratic
equation (\ref{rev:quadratic_equation}).

According to the quadratic equation, the solutions of the equation $ax^{2} + bx
+ c = 0$ are as follows:

\begin{equation}
  \label{eqt:quadratic_equation_2}
  \begin{split}
    x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^{2} - 4ac}}{2a} \\
  \end{split}
.\end{equation}

\begin{example}
  \label{exm:find_solutions}

  We must find the solutions of $2x^{2} - x + 6 = 0$. For this problem, we have
  the following information:

  \begin{equation}
    \label{eqt:information}
    \begin{split}
      a = 2 \\
      b = -1 \\
      c = 6 \\
    \end{split}
  .\end{equation}

  We use these values in the quadratic equation:

  \begin{equation}
    \label{eqt:values_in_quadratic_equation}
    \begin{split}
      x &= \frac{-(-1) \pm \sqrt{(-1)^{2} - 4 \times 2 \times 6}}{2 \times 2} \\
      &= \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{-47}}{4} \\
    \end{split}
  .\end{equation}
\end{example}

\begin{note}
  \label{nte:discriminant_of_the_following_quadratic_equation}

  The discriminant (\ref{def:discriminant}), $b^{2} - 4ac = -47$, is negative.
  So, our solutions are not real numbers. We get the following:

  \begin{equation}
    \label{eqt:non_real_number_answer_for_discriminant}
    \begin{split}
      x &= \frac{1 \pm \sqrt{47}i}{4} \\
      &= \frac{1}{4} + \frac{\sqrt{47}}{4}i
    \end{split}
  .\end{equation}

  So, our final answer is:

  \begin{equation}
    \label{eqt:final_answer_for_quadratic_equation_with_non_real_answer}
    \begin{split}
      x &= \frac{1}{4} + \frac{\sqrt{47}}{4}i \qquad\qquad \frac{1}{4} - \frac{\sqrt{47}}{4}i
    \end{split}
  .\end{equation}
\end{note}
