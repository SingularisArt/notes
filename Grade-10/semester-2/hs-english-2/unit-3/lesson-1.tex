\subsubsection{Enhancing our Vocabulary}
\label{sub_sub_sec:enhancing_our_vocabulary}

\begin{definition}[Root]
  \label{def:root}

  The fundamental or essential part of the word.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Suffixes]
  \label{def:suffixes}

  An attachment placed after a root (\ref{def:root}) word.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[Prefixes]
  \label{def:prefixes}

  An attachment placed before a root (\ref{def:root}) word.
\end{definition}

At least half of the words in the English language are derived from Greek and
Latin roots. Knowing these roots can help us figure out the meanings of words
that may be composed of roots (\ref{def:root}), suffixes (\ref{def:suffixes}),
and prefixes (\ref{def:prefixes}).

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|l|l|l|l|}
      \hline
      Prefix & Root & Suffix & Complete Word \\
      \hline
      pre- (Before) & jud (Judge) & -ice (quality of) & prejudice \\
      \hline
      & psycho (soul, spirit, m)  & -logy (study of) & psychology \\
      \hline
      re- (again) & act (do, drive, move) & -ion (the result of) & reaction \\
      \hline
      & aero (flying, planes) & -phobia (irrational fear) & aerophobia \\
      \hline
      claustr- (confined spaces) & phob (fear) & -phobia (irrational fear) &
      claustrophic \\
      \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

Some words are more than words. They're whole ideas. Choosing just the right
word can make a big difference.

\begin{center}
    \begin{tabular}{|l|r|}
      \hline
      \textbf{Common Suffixes and their Meaning} & \textbf{Common Words Using the Suffix} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-logy}: Study the field of & \textit{Biology, Technology} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ite}: One connected with & \textit{Unite, Satellite} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ia}: Sate of, or Act & \textit{Hysteria, Anesthesia} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ic}: Having to do with, Pertaining to & \textit{Epidemic, Metric} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ism}: Belief in, Practice of & \textit{Patriotism, Optimism} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ly}: Characteristic of & \textit{Quickly, Experimentally} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ful}: Full of & Careful, Beautiful \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-ist}: One who believes in or engaged in & \textit{Dermatologist, Scientist} \\
      \hline
      \textbf{-y}: Characterized by & \textit{Happy, Filthy} \\
      \hline
    \end{tabular}
\end{center}

Phobia can be used as a root (\ref{def:root}) or a suffix (\ref{def:root})
depending on the word. In the word \textbf{\textit{Claustrophobic}}, phob is
used as a root (\ref{def:root}). The prefix (\ref{def:prefixes}) "claustr" comes
before it, and the suffix (\ref{def:suffixes}) "ic" follows it. In the word
\textbf{\textit{Panophobia}}, phobia is a suffix (\ref{def:suffixes}) because it
comes after the root (\ref{def:root}) "pan".
