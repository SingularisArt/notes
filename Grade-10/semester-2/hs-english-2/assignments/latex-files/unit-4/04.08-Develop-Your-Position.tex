People love to debate on whether Brutus is a traitor for killing Caesar, or
if he's a patriot for doing what he thinks is right to save Rome. Let's start
here. What exactly is a traitor. The definition of the word \textbf{Traitor}
means: \textit{"A person who betrays a friend, country, principle, etc."} and
the definition for the word \textbf{Patriot} is:
\textit{"A person who vigorously supports their country and is prepared to
  defend it against enemies or detractors."}
People love to say that Brutus was a traitor, but if you really look closely in
the act, you will see that in the end, he kills himself because he notices that
he starts to deviate from his mission and how other people are starting to react
because of what he did. So, he ends up killing himself because he think that
\textbf{Antony} is a better leader than him, meaning, he killed himself to save
Rome. That fits the definition of the word \textbf{Patriot}. He supports his
country so much so, he talks his friend's life, thinking it would help save
Rome, and then after realizing what he has done, he talks his life to save Rome.
