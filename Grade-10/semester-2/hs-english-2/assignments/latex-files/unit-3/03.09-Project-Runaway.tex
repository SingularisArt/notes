Eye Contact deservers $4 \frac{1}{2}$ out of $5$ stars. The reason is because I
liked the intro. For me, it created great suspense and made wonder what was
going to happen. For example, in the movie \textit{Cujo}, by
\textit{Stephen King}, this exact thing happened. The location of the film
seems to be next to a sea/ocean and right next to the lighthouse. Apparently, a
women is being followed around by a shadow.

The camera angles add to the suspense of the film. It first shows us the shadow
man before letting the main character see it. I think this is a great way for
creating suspense because we have a leverage over the character, which makes us
get scared because they don't know and the shadow man might do something before
she can find out. I think another great way this movie has created suspense is
by making the shadow man mysteries. The author hid what he is/doing in the first
two videos, which made me really excited to see what he is and what he's doing.
Overall, I think the way the movie is creating suspense is very affective.

Overall, I think this short film quality is pretty good. The strengths of this
short film are the camera shots. It makes you feel like you're seeing how the
shadow man sees. I don't have any suggestions for any improvement. This short
film reminds me of the movie \textit{Cujo} for some reason. I just get this
nostalgia when watching this film.
