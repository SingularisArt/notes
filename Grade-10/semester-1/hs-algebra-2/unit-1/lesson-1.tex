\subsubsection{Rational Exponents to Radical Expressions}
\label{sub_sub_sec:rational_exponents_to_radical_expressions}

Here's the basic expression:

\[ x^{\frac{z}{y}} = \sqrt[y]{x^{a}} \].

\begin{example}
  Rewrite $(6x)^{\frac{4}{5}}$ as a radical expression. Now, let's just use that
  expression from above to solve this:

  \[ \left(6x\right)^{\frac{4}{5}} = 6\sqrt[5]{x^{4}} \].
\end{example}

\subsubsection{Radical Expressions to Rational Exponents}
\label{sub_sub_sec:radical_expressions_to_rational_exponents}

Now, let's do the reverse. Like before, here's the basic expression:

\[ \sqrt[y]{x^{z}} = x^{\frac{z}{y}} \].

\begin{example}
  Let's rewrite $\sqrt[8]{w^{5}}$ as a rational expression:

  \[ \sqrt[8]{x^{5}} = x^{\frac{5}{8}} \].
\end{example}
