\begin{definition}[Radical Equations]
  Here is what a \textbf{Radical Equation} looks like:

  \[ T = 2\pi\sqrt{\frac{L}{32}} .\]
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[Radical Equations 1]
  Let's solve $\sqrt{(5x + 4)} = 7$:
  
  \begin{align*}
    \sqrt{(5x + 4)} = 7 \\
    (\sqrt{5x + 4})^2 = (7)^2 \\
    5x + 4 = 49 \\
    5x = 45 \\
    \frac{5x}{5} = \frac{45}{5} \\
    x = 9
  .\end{align*}
  
  Now, let's check our work:
  
  \begin{align*}
    \sqrt{5x + 4} = 7 \\
    \sqrt{5(9) + 4} = 7 \\
    \sqrt{45 + 4} = 7 \\
    \sqrt{49} = 7 \\
    7 = 7
  .\end{align*}
\end{example}

Let's try another one:

\begin{example}[Radical Equations 2]
  Let's solve $\sqrt{x - 3} + 4 = 1$:

  \begin{align*}
    \sqrt{x - 3} + 4 = 1 \\
    \sqrt{x - 3} = -3 \\
    (\sqrt{x - 3})^2 = (-3)^2 \\
    x - 3 = 9 \\
    x = 12
  .\end{align*}

  Now, let's check our work:

  \begin{align*}
    \sqrt{x - 3} + 4 = 1 \\
    \sqrt{12 - 3} + 4 = 1 \\
    \sqrt{9} + 4 = 1 \\
    3 + 4 = 1 \\
    7 = 1 \textrm{ No Solution :(}
  .\end{align*}

  We call this situation extraneous solution.
\end{example}

\subsection*{Quick Review of Factoring}
\label{sub_sec:quick_review_of_factoring}

Let's look at factoring by group.

First, you want the split the middle term into factors of $15$ that combine to equal $2$.

Then, just factor by $GCF$.

\begin{align*}
  x^2 + 2x - 15 \\
  x^2 - 3x + 5x - 15 \\
  (x^2 - 3x) + (5x - 15) \\
  x(x - 3) + 5(x - 3) \\
  (x + 5)(x - 3)
.\end{align*}
