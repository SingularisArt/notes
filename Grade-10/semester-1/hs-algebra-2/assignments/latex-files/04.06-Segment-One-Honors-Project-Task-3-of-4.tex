\begin{question}[Determine how many, what type, and find the roots for: \\
  $f(x) = x^{4} + 21x^2 - 100$]

  Firstly, there are going to be $4$ roots, because the highest degree is $4$.

  Now, to find the roots, let's first factor:

  \begin{align}
    f(x)  &= x^{4} + 21x^2 - 100 \\
          &= x^{4} - 4x^2 + 25x^2 - 100 \\
          &= (x^2 + 25) \times (x^2 - 4) \\
          &= \boxed{(x^2 + 25)(x + 2)(x - 2)}
  .\end{align}

  Now, let's find the roots:

  \begin{align}
    x^2 + 25    &= 0             \\
    x^2         &= -25           \\
    \sqrt{x^2}  &= \sqrt{-25}            \\
    x           &= 5i, -5i      \\
    x + 2       &= 0            \\
    x           &= -2           \\
    x - 2       &= 0            \\
    x           &= 2
  .\end{align}

  So, here are the roots:
  \[ \pm 2; \pm 5i .\]
\end{question}

\begin{question}[determine how many, what type, and find the roots for: \\
  $f(x) = x^3 - 5x^2 - 25x + 125$]

  Firstly, there are going to be $3$ roots, because the highest degree is $3$.

  Now, to find the roots, let's first factor:

  \begin{align}
    f(x)  &= x^3 - 5x^2 - 25x + 125 \\
          &= (x^3 - 5^2) + (-25x + 125) \\
          &= x^2(x - 5) - 25(x - 5) \\
          &= (x - 5)(x + 5)(x - 5) \\
          &= (x - 5)^2 + (x + 5) \\
  .\end{align}

  Now, let's find the roots:

  \begin{align}
    (x - 5)^2       &= 0 \\
    \sqrt{(x - 5)^2}    &= 0 \\
    x - 5          &= 0 \\
    x              &= 5 \\
    x + 5          &= 0 \\
    x              &= -5 \\
  .\end{align}

  So, here are the roots:

  \[ \pm 5 .\]
\end{question}
