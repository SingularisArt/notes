\begin{problem}
  When I was younger, about $8 - 9$ years old, I enjoyed watching shows like
  \textbf{Popular Mechanics}, \textbf{National Geographic}, \textbf{etc}. But I
  regretted watching one of the \textbf{National Geographic} videos for almost
  $2$ years. I learned about sinkholes, what they are, and how they're formed. I
  was terrified. The video that terrified me the most was the video with a
  sinkhole with noise coming out. From that day on, I imagined it was the end of
  times. Everyday, I would act as if it was my last day, spend countless hours
  reading about sinkholes, how they're formed. So much so, I bothered everybody
  to the point that they didn't allow me to read anymore. But, that didn't stop
  me. I reached the point where I couldn't sleep well. Every time I closed my
  eyes, I would hear babel from the sinkholes and visually see it as if I was
  falling in one. Eventually, like all kids, I grew out of the phobia.

  % Paragraph 1: Talk about the story: The Premature Burial by Edgar Allan Poe
  One story that I read really resonated with me. It's \textit{Edgar Allan
  Poe's} story called \textbf{The Premature Burial}. \textit{Victorine
  Lafourcade} describes his utmost fear of being buried alive because of her
  condition, \textbf{catalepsy}. She does everything in his power to prevent from
  being prematurely buried. She eventually goes crazy because of this fear and
  all of the people who loved or knew eventually leave him and she's isolated
  from the real world. Ironically, she does end up being buried alive.

  % Paragraph 3: Talk about the movie: Train to Busan by Yeon Sang-ho
  The movie \textbf{Train to Busan} by \textit{Yeon Sang-ho} is a Korean movie
  which is set at a time when there's an outbreak. I would not categorize this
  movie like all the other apocalyptic movies. This movie criticizes society and
  the producer wanted everyone to see what society is for what it really is. The
  movie mostly focuses on individualism, one, wealthy, powerful person in charge
  and everybody else just following along like sheep. I think this movie really
  defines what the term \textbf{Apocalyptic} means, which is \textbf{Apocalyptic
  events bring out the true color of individuals, societies, and even countries
  and the only people who survive are the people who leach off of others.}
\end{problem}
